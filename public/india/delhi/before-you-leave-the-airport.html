Before You Leave the Airport

Updated: June 7, 2026

You have landed. The aircraft has taxied to its gate with all the urgency of a retired postman on a Sunday stroll, and the seatbelt sign has barely flickered off before half the cabin is already on its feet, yanking open overhead bins with the focus of surgeons in an operating theatre. Welcome to the opening act.

Before you step outside these air-conditioned walls and into the great wide open of Delhi, there are a few things to sort. Think of the airport as a decompression chamber between the world you know and the one that’s waiting for you on the other side of the sliding glass doors. Use the time wisely.
